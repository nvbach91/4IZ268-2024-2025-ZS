About Elon Musk

Elon Musk is a visionary entrepreneur, inventor, and engineer. Known for founding companies like Tesla, SpaceX, Neuralink, and The Boring Company, Musk has redefined industries and inspired millions worldwide.

Biography

Elon Musk was born in Pretoria, South Africa, in 1971. Fascinated by technology and innovation, he taught himself programming at the age of 10. In 1995, he moved to the United States to attend Stanford University but left after just two days to pursue entrepreneurial ventures. From co-founding Zip2 to creating revolutionary companies like Tesla and SpaceX, Musk has become one of the most influential figures in modern technology.

His ambition extends beyond Earth, aiming to make humanity a multi-planetary species through SpaceX. Musk is also committed to sustainable energy solutions, developing electric vehicles and solar power technologies to combat climate change.
