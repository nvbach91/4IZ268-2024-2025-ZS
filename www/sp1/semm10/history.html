The History of Gorillaz

The Birth of Gorillaz (1998–2000)

Gorillaz was born out of a creative partnership between Damon Albarn and Jamie Hewlett in 1998. The idea emerged during a shared flat in London, where the two envisioned a virtual band as a critique of the manufactured nature of pop music and celebrity culture. Albarn brought his musical expertise, while Hewlett, famed for his work on Tank Girl, developed the unique animated characters that would become the face of the band: 2-D, Murdoc Niccals, Noodle, and Russel Hobbs.

In 2000, Gorillaz debuted with the single “Clint Eastwood,” a genre-blending track that captured global attention. The band's self-titled debut album followed in 2001, introducing the world to an innovative concept that combined music, animation, and storytelling. This groundbreaking project set the stage for Gorillaz’s future success.

Rising to Fame: Demon Days Era (2001–2006)

Following the success of their debut album, Gorillaz entered a new phase of creativity with the release of Demon Days in 2005. Produced by Danger Mouse, the album was a darker, more introspective exploration of global issues such as war, environmental decay, and human disconnection.

Tracks like “Feel Good Inc.” and “DARE” became international hits, solidifying Gorillaz’s status as a global phenomenon. Their innovative music videos and live performances, featuring holographic projections of the animated members, captivated audiences worldwide. This era marked a peak in the band’s cultural impact, with Demon Days becoming one of the most critically acclaimed albums of the decade.

Experimentation and Evolution (2010–2018)

After a hiatus, Gorillaz returned in 2010 with Plastic Beach, a concept album tackling environmental issues and consumer culture. This project saw the band working with an eclectic mix of collaborators, including Lou Reed, Snoop Dogg, and Bobby Womack. The album’s narrative expanded the Gorillaz universe, introducing fans to the fictional Plastic Beach island.

In 2011, the band released The Fall, a more intimate and experimental album recorded during their tour. Following another break, Gorillaz re-emerged in 2017 with Humanz, a vibrant, politically charged album that reflected the chaotic state of the world. The next year, The Now Now offered a more personal and stripped-down sound, showcasing the band’s ability to adapt and evolve.

A New Chapter: Song Machine and Beyond (2020–Present)

In 2020, Gorillaz launched the Song Machine project, releasing tracks episodically throughout the year. The album, Song Machine, Season One: Strange Timez, featured a diverse roster of collaborators, including Robert Smith, Elton John, and St. Vincent, and embraced a fresh, experimental approach to music production.

In 2023, the band released Cracker Island, continuing their tradition of innovation and storytelling. With its funky grooves and star-studded collaborations, the album showcased Gorillaz's relevance in the modern musical landscape.

Today, Gorillaz remains a pioneer in the music industry, blending technology, art, and sound to create immersive experiences. Their ongoing journey continues to inspire fans worldwide, proving that Gorillaz is more than just a band—it’s a living, evolving work of art.
