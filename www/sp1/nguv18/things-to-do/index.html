Food and drink

Main dishes

Phở

A must-try dish when visiting Hanoi is Pho, one of Vietnam's most beloved breakfast foods. Each region in Vietnam has its unique way of preparing and serving it, but the Hanoi version is something you cannot miss.

Phở bò

Phở bò Hà Nội (Hanoi beef pho) consists of pho, broth, beef, and herbs. Unlike pho in Saigon, Hanoi pho is not served with fresh herbs (rau sống). The beef is typically either rare or well-done. A bowl of beef pho is never complete without the crispy quẩy (fried dough). Here are a few of my favorite pho spots:

  • Phở Long Bích:
    1. 121 Tam Trinh street
    2. 96 Võ Thị Sáu street
    3. 6 Bằng Liệt street
  • Phở Anh Béo: the intersection of Nguyen Khac Hieu Street and Tran The Xuong Street, near Truc Bach Lake.
Phở gà

Besides phở bò, you can also enjoy phở gà (chicken pho). Similar to phở bò, phở gà is served with the addition of lime leaves(lá chanh) – a must-have when eating with boiled chicken. Here are a few of my favorite spots:

  • Phở gà Vân Thủy: 168 Lò Đúc street
  • Phở Gà Ta Hương: 35 Tô Hiến Thành street
  • Phở gà bún thang Thủy: 22 Nam Tràng street

Bún chả

This is a must-try lunch in Hanoi. It is served with Nem rán(spring rolls) and fresh herbs. I would recommend you try it in a street food vendor - one of the traditional things in Vietnam.
To try the old taste of Bún chả Hà Nội, you should come to Bún chả que che: 16A Nguyễn Khắc Nhu Street.

Bún thang

Bún thang is a dish unique to Hanoi. You can find it in some small restaurant that have phở gà.
My recommedation is Phở gà bún thang Thủy: 22 Nam Tràng street

Snack

Besides typical main dishes, Hanoi people love snacks so much. It is not an official tradition, but parents often buy them light snacks after picking up kids from school. Apart from the traditional snacks such as Cháo sườn, Quẩy, Bánh rán,... Several new snacks worldwide, like Takoyaki, Hawker Star, Donuts, and Kebab, are imported to Hanoi. Also, Vietnamese snacks are often in small sizes, so they won't affect your meal.

Drinks and bevarages

As well as snacks, there are plenty of options for drinks in Hanoi. Vietnam is famous for coffee, and it has reached another level. It is not much when everyone says that you can't sleep if you drink Vietnamese in the afternoon. An Alternative option is egg coffee. It is a unique type of coffee that you should try.
Besides that, you can have several options like fresh bia (bia hơi), dracontomelon juice (nước sấu), sugar cane juice (nước mía), or sidewalk iced tea (trà đá vỉa hè).

Cultural activities

Water puppets

Water puppets are a traditional Vietnamese art form performed on a water-filled stage in the 11th century. Each sense tells stories from rural life and folklore. The puppets are controlled by skilled puppeteers standing behind a screen, with music and narration enhancing the performance. It’s a unique cultural experience and a must-see for visitors to Hanoi.

Vietnam Museum of Ethnology

The Vietnam Museum of Ethnology in Hanoi is a captivating place to discover the cultural richness of Vietnam’s 54 ethnic groups. During Tet (Vietnamese New Year), the museum hosts special activities where visitors can experience traditional games or crafts like making tò he figurines and Dong Ho folk paintings while enjoying minority cuisines. These events provide a unique glimpse into Vietnam’s vibrant heritage and holiday traditions.

Vietnam National Museum of History

The Vietnam National Museum of History, located in Hanoi, offers a comprehensive journey through the nation's rich past. The museum houses thousands of artifacts, from ancient relics and bronze drums (Trống đồng Đông Sơn) to items from Vietnam's feudal dynasties and revolutionary period. Its stunning French colonial architecture adds charm, making it a must-visit for history enthusiasts. Visitors can explore Vietnam's cultural and historical evolution, gaining a deeper appreciation of its heritage.
