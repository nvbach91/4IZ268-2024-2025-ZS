Tourist destination

Hanoi's Old Quarter

Old Quarter is the center of Hanoi. Sometimes, it is even called the name Hà Nội 36 phố phường (36 old quarters). The streets are named uniquely after the specific goods that they sold in the past. For instance, Hang Bac was known for silver crafting, or Hang Muoi for salt. While some roads have preserved their traditional crafts, others have adapted to modern life. Some part of the Old Quarter's architecture, influenced by late 19th-century French styles, features 1-2 story houses with moss-covered red-tile roofs, creating a nostalgic and poetic atmosphere.

The Imperial Citadel of Thang Long

The Imperial Citadel of Thang Long (Hoàng thành Thăng Long) is an iconic structure built in 1010 during the Ly Dynasty. It is a significant architectural and historical achievement, initiated by Ly Cong Uan (Ly Thai To) immediately after he moved the capital of Dai Viet (old Vietnam) from Hoa Lu to Thang Long. Over various dynasties, the citadel was continuously expanded and developed, serving as a center of power for over a millennium.

Hoan Kiem lake

Hoan Kiem Lake ( Hồ Hoàn Kiếm), also called Hồ Gươm or "Bờ Hồ" by locals and located in the heart of the capital, is an iconic symbol of Hanoi deeply rooted in the city's history and culture. The lake is tied to the legend of King Le Loi returning a sword to the Golden Turtle, symbolizing patriotism and peace.
Beside the Turtle Tower (Tháp Rùa), Ngoc Son temple is a historic and spiritual landmark dedicated to General Tran Hung Dao, a revered Vietnamese hero. Accessible via the iconic The Huc Bridge, it offers a serene escape amidst the bustling city.
Beyond its historical significance, Hoan Kiem Lake is a place where tradition and modern life mix. Whether at sunrise or under the glow of city lights, its serene atmosphere provides a peaceful retreat from the bustling streets. It’s also a place where locals gather to exercise or enjoy the view of Hoan Kiem Lake, reflecting the unique rhythm of life in Hanoi.

Temple of Literature

Văn Miếu - Quốc Tử Giám was built in 1070 under the Ly Dynasty and became Vietnam's first university and a symbol of Vietnam's educational tradition.
A key feature of the temple is the Doctors' Stelae, 82 stone tablets on turtle backs, recording the names of scholars from 1442 to 1779. These stelae honor academic excellence and inspire future generations to value learning and dedication.
Nowadays, Khue Van Cac (the picture) is chosen as a symbol of Hanoi.

Buildings of French Architecture

Hanoi Opera House

Hanoi Opera House, built between 1901 and 1911, is a unique architectural masterpiece showcasing European elegance in the heart of the capital. Constructed with materials imported from France, it embodies refined details, from sturdy columns to graceful arches, symbolizing East-West cultural harmony.
The Hanoi Opera House is chosen to hold significant events and essential ceremonies in Vietnam as a symbol of cultural and historical significance.

Long Bien bridge

Built between 1899 and 1902 by France's Daydé & Pillé company and designed by Gustave Eiffel, Long Bien Bridge is an architectural masterpiece tied to Hanoi's history. As the first steel bridge spanning the Red River, it has witnessed the city's struggles and transformations over time.
From here, you can enjoy stunning views of the Red River at sunrise or sunset, with light reflecting on the water, creating a romantic scene.

Hanoi Cathedral

St. Joseph's Cathedral in Hanoi, built-in 1886 during French rule, is a Gothic icon inspired by Notre Dame de Paris. With soaring arches, vibrant stained glass, and sturdy pillars, it serves as both a key Catholic site and a beloved tourist destination, symbolizing Hanoi's timeless charm.
During December, especially around Christmas, the Cathedral attracts enormous crowds, including Christians, locals and tourists who come to enjoy the festive decorations, music, and vibrant holiday atmosphere.

Places near Hanoi

Incense Village - Quảng Phú Cầu

Quang Phu Cau incense village in Hanoi’s Ung Hoa district is famed for its centuries-old incense-making tradition. Especially busy during Tet, villagers craft vibrant red incense sticks for spiritual practices nationwide. It preserves cultural heritage and attracts both local and international visitors as a unique day-trip destination near Hanoi.

Sapa

Sapa, a serene mountain town in northern Vietnam, is famous for its terraced rice fields and rich cultural heritage. It's also the gateway to Fansipan, the highest peak in Vietnam.
FFansipan, the "Roof of Indochina," stands at 3,143m and is the highest peak not only in Vietnam but also in the Indochinese peninsula. Visitors can conquer it via modern cable car or adventurous trekking, enjoying stunning views of majestic terraced fields. Don't forget to prepare warm clothes for the trip to "the Roof of Indochina."

Ha Long bay

Ha Long Bay - "Descending Dragon" is one of Vietnam's most famous natural wonders, with majestic landscapes and unique formations like emerald waters, limestone islands, and mystical caves. Visitors can enjoy luxury experiences aboard five-star boats, with gourmet dining, spa services, and breathtaking bay views. The locals provide activities like kayaking through hidden lagoons and exploring floating fishing villages.

Ninh Binh

Co Do Hoa Lu was Dai Co Viet (old Vietnam)'s first capital under the Dinh and Hau Le dynasties. It is a historic site surrounded by majestic mountains that symbolize strength and prosperity. This place was chosen as the capital before Hanoi.
Trang An is another beautiful place to visit in Ninh Binh. Visitors can enjoy peaceful boat rides and explore historic sites, including the ancient Tran wine cellars, a key part of Vietnam's heritage. The journey through towering mountains and serene waters offers stunning views. Majestic temples and pagodas stand on the cliffs, combining spiritual beauty and historical significance, immersing travelers in a sacred, mysterious world.

Transportation

  • Taxi

    Taxi is the most popular mode of transport due to its affordability and quick travel time in Hanoi. The rise of ride-hailing services like Grab or Be has increased the convenience of traveling.

  • Motorbike

    Motorbikes are the most widespread means of transportation for riding around Hanoi. Visitors can rent a motorbike with a driving license, but it's better to go with a local to get accustomed to Hanoi's unique traffic patterns.

  • Bus

    Apart from regular buses riding around the city, Hanoi offers double-decker buses for sightseeing. These buses go around the city center and stop at tourist attractions.
