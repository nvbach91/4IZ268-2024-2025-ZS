Seasons

Spring

Srping is the first season of the year. It is warmer than winter, but it rains lightly several days. So the moisture in the atmosphere is rather high. However, in this season, we celebrate the biggest holiday of the year.
Tet holiday, Tet Nguyen Dan or Lunar New Year is a biggest traditional Vietnamese celebration. If you plan to visit Hanoi during spring, don't miss this holiday.A pro tip is when you see shops full of people, you should buy food for at least 3 days. Because it can be the time, when everyone is preparing for the Tet holiday as shops may close during this period. You can ask the local in order to know the date exactly since is caclutated in lunar calendar.For an unforgetable experience, you should consider celebrating Tet with locals in nearby places like Sapa or Mai Chau.

Summer

Summer is the hottest season of the year. It is the season to have food and drink that has light taste like gỏi cuốn (spring rolls), bánh mì (Vietnamese sandwich), and chè (dessert soups). During this season, it's best to head to the mountains, like Sapa, Tam Dao, or Mai Chau, to escape the heat. Also, you can visit urban beaches or enjoy water parks for a refreshing experience like Vinhomes Ocean Park or VinWonders Wave Park . Those places are not so far from the city center.

Autumn

Autumn in Hanoi is the most beautiful season of the year. Therefore, it is the best time to go, with cool, pleasant weather and gentle sunshine. You can wear short sleeves but you should bring a light jacket for the chilly mornings and late evenings. You can experience a city tour on a double-decker bus or try to be a local by enjoying breakfast with a bowl of pho in the early morning , then taking a motorcycle ride around the Old Quarter. And last but not least, don’t forget to stop by a café with a great view.
Mid-Autumn Festival, celebrated in Vietnam and other parts of Asia, is a traditional harvest festival held on the 15th day of the 8th lunar month. It’s a time for families to gather, enjoy mooncakes, and admire the full moon. Children often participate in lantern processions, and various cultural performances take place. The best place to enjoy Mid-Autmn Festival is Hang Ma street. The whole street are full of shops with decoration. You can find the traditional toys as the most mordern one in this street.

Winter

Hanoi’s winter is not as cold as in other countries. There won't shnow but are still chilly days, so it's important to bring warm clothing. To stay cozy, try local delicacies like lục tào phớ (a sweet tofu dessert) or bánh trôi tàu (sticky rice dumplings). If you prefer warmer weather, you can visit south to escape the cold.
Christmas is not our main holdiay, but we also decorate accoridng to the Christmas theme. You can Hanoi Cathedral to enjoy the atmosphere or come by Hang Ma street to get some decoration or gifts from your famliy and friends.

Shopping

Night Market

The Dong Xuan Night Market, located in the heart of Hanoi's Old Quarter, is a vibrant destination that comes alive every Friday and weekend evening from 7 PM. The market offers a wide variety of goods, from delicious street food to everyday essentials. It’s also a great place to find unique Don’t about communication, because most vendors speak some English, so it is fine for you to shop and explore.

Shopping malls

There are several shopping mall centers in Hanoi. Near the Old Quarter, there is Vincom Ba Trieu. Lotte Mall and Aeon Mall are a bit further from the city center but it has more shops for your choice.

Small shops

Hanoi locals often shop at small stores along the streets. You can also come by these shops and you might find some interesting and unique items for your trip.
